# AI Observability: Revolutionizing Business Alerting in BI Platform

At Volkov Labs, we’re dedicated to redefining Business Intelligence (BI) by integrating cutting-edge technologies that deliver actionable insights and exceptional user experiences. Our latest innovation—AI Observability—transforms how businesses manage critical alerts within our BI platform.

By harnessing advanced AI models like Grok 4 from xAI and Anthropic Claude, we’ve reimagined [Business Alerting](/big/alerting/) to provide dynamic, context-rich notifications across email, Slack, and Grafana Incident workflows. This blog post dives into how AI Observability enhances alerting, empowering teams to respond faster and smarter to critical issues.

## The Problem: Static Alerts in a Dynamic Landscape

Traditional alerting systems in BI platforms often rely on static templates and fixed thresholds. While functional, these systems lack the adaptability and depth required for today’s fast-paced business environments. Alerts frequently miss critical context, offering little beyond a basic notification. This leaves teams to manually investigate issues, wasting time and risking delayed responses or missed opportunities.

Consider a performance alert for a Grafana dashboard metric like **Grafana - Dashboard Get** on an instance named **alpha1**. A conventional alert might simply read, _"Response time exceeds 400 ms on alpha1."_ While this signals a problem, it fails to explain the severity, implications, or next steps, forcing teams to dig deeper on their own.

## Case Study: Grafana Dashboard Performance Alert

Imagine a performance metric for the "Grafana - Dashboard Get" API on the "alpha1" instance triggers an alert. The response time is 406.75 ms, surpassing the warning threshold of 400 ms. In a traditional setup, the alert might be a vague, one-line message. With AI Observability, it becomes a comprehensive, AI-generated report tailored to the event data.

Here’s an example of the notification:

:::info AI-Generated Notification
**Subject: [Alert] Grafana Dashboard Performance Issue on alpha1**

**Alert Title**: Grafana - Dashboard Get  
**Instance**: alpha1 (Job: alpha-grafana-1)  
**Current State**: Alerting (Triggered on 2025-07-14T13:55:03.862Z)  
**Issue**: The average response time for dashboard GET API calls on instance "alpha1" is 406.75 ms, exceeding the warning threshold of 400 ms.

**Details**:

- **Metric**: Rate of total latency divided by request count over a 1-minute interval.
- **Thresholds**: Green (< 400 ms), Warning (≥ 400 ms), Critical (≥ 600 ms).
- **Sampled Data**: Consistent response time of 406.75 ms across recent intervals.

**Potential Implications**:

1. **Performance Degradation**: Minor latency increases may slow dashboard loading, impacting user experience.
2. **User Impact**: Sustained delays could frustrate users who depend on real-time data access.
3. **System Health**: This may signal resource constraints or inefficient dashboard queries.
4. **Risk of Escalation**: If response times continue to rise, a critical alert (≥ 600 ms) could be triggered.

**Recommendations**:

- Investigate "alpha1" performance metrics and logs for root causes (e.g., resource usage, network issues).
- Monitor trends to assess if latency persists or worsens.
- Optimize dashboard queries or scale resources if load is a factor.
- Reassess the 400 ms warning threshold for alignment with performance goals.

**Action**: Address the "alpha1" instance promptly to prevent escalation and ensure optimal user experience.
:::

This AI-crafted message can be delivered via email for detailed records, posted to a Slack channel for team collaboration, or integrated into a Grafana Incident workflow for structured resolution. The depth of insight and clear guidance empower teams to act decisively.

## How AI Observability Powers Our Platform

Our AI Observability integration within the Business Intelligence platform follows a streamlined process:

1. **Event Payload Capture**: When an alert triggers, the system collects comprehensive data, including metrics, thresholds, timestamps, and dashboard variables.
2. **AI Analysis**: Advanced models process the payload, identifying patterns, assessing severity, and crafting human-readable notifications with actionable insights.
3. **Dynamic Notification Creation**: The AI generates tailored messages, adapting tone and format to suit the delivery channel while highlighting key details and recommendations.
4. **Multi-Channel Delivery**: Notifications are sent via email for formal updates, Slack for real-time collaboration, or Grafana Incident for incident tracking.
5. **Continuous Improvement**: Teams can provide feedback on AI-generated messages, helping refine the model’s accuracy and relevance over time.
